What is the Age-Friendly Northvale Initiative?

Launched in 2019, the Age-Friendly Northvale Initiative was designed to make Northvale a great place to grow older. It was structured around three focus areas:

Outdoor Spaces and Housing — Neighbourhoods and homes that are safe, accessible, and welcoming for people of all ages and abilities.

Social and Civic Participation — A city where older residents have every opportunity to stay engaged, contribute, and play an active role in community life.

Information and Services — A future where everyone can easily find, access, and navigate the services and support they need, when they need them.

These focus areas were supported by key enablers:

01

Challenging ageism to build a culture of inclusion

02

Improving accessibility so everyone can participate fully

03

Strengthening partnerships between council, community organisations, and services

How was the review conducted?

The review was guided by an evaluation framework developed with Northvale City Council and co-designed with older residents, community organisations, and service providers to ensure findings reflect real experiences.

This impact review assesses what has been achieved over five years, identifies areas for further development, and lays the groundwork for the next Age-Friendly Northvale Strategy.

Outdoor Spaces and Housing

Building a city where everyone can live and move freely

Priority Summary

Creating an age-friendly city starts with the physical environment. Older residents need safe footpaths, well-maintained parks, accessible transport, and housing that adapts as their needs change. Many Northvale residents feel proud of their neighbourhoods, but not everyone benefits equally. Outer suburb residents, people with mobility needs, and renters face distinct barriers that limit their ability to stay active and connected in their communities.

Key Results

What's working well?

Safe Neighbourhoods

8 in 10 older Northvale residents feel safe in their local neighbourhood.

Accessible Open Spaces

Around 7 in 10 say they can access local parks and open spaces without difficulty.

Well-Maintained Streets

"The footpaths in my street have improved so much — I feel so much safer walking to the shops."

Community Survey respondent, Inner Northvale

Most residents are happy with council maintenance of local streets and footpaths.

Key Results

What are the challenges?

Transport Gaps

1 in 3 outer suburb residents say public transport doesn't meet their needs, limiting access to services and social activities.

Housing Shortfall

"There's nothing out there that's affordable and actually accessible."

Community Survey respondent

Affordable, accessible housing remains limited, particularly for renters and people with disabilities.

Inconsistent Design

"One end of my street is fine. A block away and it's a completely different story."

Focus group participant

Age-friendly design principles are inconsistently applied across new developments and public infrastructure.

Key takeaway

Northvale's public spaces are valued, but access is unequal. Outer suburb residents and people with mobility needs face real barriers to independence. Embedding age-friendly design into planning and transport decisions is essential for the next phase.

Social and Civic Participation

Supporting older residents to stay engaged and contribute

Priority Summary

Staying socially connected and civically engaged is central to ageing well. Northvale's older residents are active contributors — volunteering, joining community groups, and taking part in local events. But not everyone has the same opportunities. Transport limitations, ageist attitudes, and a lack of age-inclusive programming can make it harder to stay involved, particularly after major life changes like retirement or bereavement.

Key Results

What's working well?

Feeling Included

4 in 5 older residents feel welcomed and included in community activities.

Sense of Purpose

6 in 10 feel they have meaningful ways to contribute to their local community.

Volunteering Culture

"I've been volunteering here for fifteen years. It's what gets me out of bed."

Community Survey respondent

Older residents make up the largest volunteer cohort across the city, contributing thousands of hours each year.

Key Results

What are the challenges?

Transport Barriers

Only 1 in 3 outer suburb residents say transport options meet their needs for social and cultural participation.

Ageist Attitudes

"Sometimes you can tell people just don't expect much from you because of your age."

Focus group participant

Ageist attitudes affect how some older residents experience community spaces and opportunities to participate in local decision-making.

Social Isolation

"After my husband passed, weeks would go by without a real conversation."

Focus group participant

Social isolation remains a real risk, especially for residents who lose regular routines and relationships after major life transitions.

Key takeaway

Older Northvale residents want to stay active and contribute, but transport gaps and ageism can limit full participation. Creating genuinely inclusive communities — online and in-person — is what makes the difference.

Information and Services

Making it easier to find, access, and navigate support

Priority Summary

Knowing what support is available — and being able to access it — is fundamental to ageing well. Many Northvale residents manage complex service systems at the same time. While community organisations play a vital role in helping people navigate, the landscape can feel fragmented. Digital barriers and low awareness mean some older residents miss out on support they are entitled to.

Key Results

What's working well?

Community as Navigator

"The people at my local centre just know everything. I wouldn't know where else to start."

Community Survey respondent

Community organisations are the most used and trusted source of service information for older Northvale residents.

Service Confidence

6 in 10 feel confident accessing council services and information when they know where to look.

Program Awareness

"There's always something on. I just check the noticeboard at the centre."

Community Survey respondent

The majority of older residents are aware of at least one council-funded ageing program in their area.

Key Results

What are the challenges?

Not Person-Centred

Fewer than 2 in 5 feel services truly reflect their individual needs and circumstances.

Digital Exclusion

"Everything is online now. If you can't do that, you just miss out."

Focus group participant

Many residents, particularly those over 75, struggle to access information and services online, missing support they are entitled to.

Fragmented Services

"You get passed from one place to the next and nobody seems to have the full picture."

Interview participant

Services can feel fragmented and hard to navigate, especially when support is needed across health, housing, and community systems at the same time.

Key takeaway

Community organisations are the connective tissue of the system — but digital barriers and awareness gaps mean support doesn't reach everyone who needs it. Simpler, more joined-up services would make a real difference.

What Comes Next

Review findings will shape the next phase of the Age-Friendly Northvale Initiative.

Why This Matters

From review findings to community conversations

The Impact Review, alongside a scan of age-friendly initiatives from across Australia and internationally, has surfaced persistent challenges that need deeper exploration. The next phase will engage with a range of community groups — particularly those whose voices have been underrepresented — to shape the next Age-Friendly Northvale Strategy. While thousands of residents have already contributed, further conversations are needed to ensure no one is left behind.

Community Conversations

Four themes will guide the next phase

These themes connect what the review found with where deeper community conversations are needed.

01

Health and Wellbeing

Supporting residents to maintain good health, manage chronic conditions, and access preventive care throughout later life.

02

Belonging and Connection

Strengthening social networks, encouraging volunteering and participation, and tackling digital exclusion to keep older residents engaged.

03

Getting Around

Improving transport, pedestrian infrastructure, and access to local services for older residents in all parts of Northvale.

04

Housing and Neighbourhood

Expanding accessible, affordable housing options and embedding age-friendly design into planning and development decisions.
